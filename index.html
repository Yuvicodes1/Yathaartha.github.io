Defence Electronics Application Lab (DEAL),DRDO (May '25 - Jun '25)

Summer Intern

Conducted a comprehensive research internship at the Defence Electronics Applications Laboratory (DEAL), DRDO, focusing on the critical integration of Zero Trust Architecture (ZTA) within emerging 6G wireless communication networks. Analyzed the limitations of traditional security models against advanced 6G threats and explored ZTA's principles, architectures (including NIST 800-207 and Google BeyondCorp), and migration strategies to ensure robust, future-ready cybersecurity.

  • Researched and analyzed the imperative shift from perimeter-based security to Zero Trust Architecture (ZTA)
  • Explored advanced cybersecurity trends relevant to 6G, including blockchain for identity management, AI-based trust models, and federated learning, demonstrating their synergy with ZTA.
  • Explored topics including network segmentation, identity-aware access control, cloud architecture, distributed learning and edge computing within ZTA.
  • Gained hands-on exposure to national defense research workflows.
